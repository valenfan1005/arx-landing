Quick take

The setup this week comes down to a handful of numbers.

  • Fed hike odds: roughly 32% by year-end on Polymarket as of May 18. Four FOMC dissents at the prior meeting, the most since late 1992. A hot CPI print on May 12 flipped the curve from cut-priced to hike-tilted.
  • BTC: $76,940 on Hyperliquid mark price, down 1.2% on the day. Funding APR has compressed to 3.66%, from roughly 44% a few weeks ago. The leveraged long crowd is being flushed.
  • S&P 500: closed Friday at 7,408.50, down 1.45% from Thursday’s ATH of 7,517.12. VIX at 18.43, below the long-run average. Equity options are still pricing nothing.
  • Brent crude: stuck near $104 with the Strait of Hormuz half-closed. That part is not going away on its own.

Day 80 since the US-Iran war started Feb 28. The April 8 ceasefire is on what Trump called “massive life support” after he rejected Tehran’s latest proposal. An NSC meeting this week to weigh next steps. This is the stagflation setup that bond markets have started to price and equity markets have not. Below is the read.

The stagflation setup nobody priced in

Stagflation has two ingredients: sticky inflation and slowing growth. Both are visible in the May 18 data if you read it honestly.

Sticky inflation comes from the supply side. Brent crude has held near $104 for two weeks because the Strait of Hormuz is half-closed; that is energy-driven inflation no Fed action can fix. April headline CPI came in at 3.8% year-over-year on the May 12 print, well above the Fed’s 2% target and the third upside surprise in four months.

Slowing growth is the part the equity tape is ignoring. An energy shock is mechanically GDP-negative for net importers, which includes the US, and the lead-lag between an oil shock and US earnings runs six to twelve months. We are 80 days in. That bill is coming.

What makes this distinct from a 1970s replay is the institutional response. The Fed is publicly torn: four dissents (three opposing the easing bias), incoming Chair Kevin Warsh, historically known as a hawk, has signaled a more accommodative stance recently, and the President has been publicly pushing for cuts. Sticky inflation, growth pressure, political pressure to cut, hawkish dissents inside the building — that combination is the textbook setup for a policy mistake in either direction. The ambiguity is exactly what shows up as the Fed-hike probability oscillating between 15% and 35% depending on the most recent print.

The Polymarket-implied 32% chance of a hike is not a forecast. It is a measure of how much disagreement there is.

Why BTC is sitting at $77K, not $90K

Read only the institutional flow data and Bitcoin should be at $90K. CoinShares logged $933M of weekly inflows into digital-asset investment products. Roughly 75% of surveyed institutions see BTC as undervalued at current levels, and Hyperliquid USDC supply has grown to about $5B via Coinbase’s expanded deployer role. The structural demand is there.

The leveraged demand is not. BTC perp funding on Hyperliquid sits at 3.66% APR right now; a few weeks ago that number was over 40%. This is a textbook leverage flush. The carry-trade longs who paid 40% APR to be long BTC are getting margin-called or stopped out as price grinds lower. Spot ETF buyers absorb the supply, leveraged longs provide it.

The macro headwind is real-yield-driven. When the market reprices Fed hike risk higher, real yields rise. BTC has traded as a long-duration risk asset for the last two cycles, and long-duration assets get repriced down when real yields go up. The relationship is tight enough that you can watch the 10-year TIPS yield and forecast BTC’s direction over the next 24 hours with surprising accuracy.

The HL funding signal matters here in a way it doesn’t on CEX venues. Hyperliquid pays funding hourly; CEX venues pay every 8 hours. So when the leverage picture changes (a liquidation cascade, a wave of stop-outs), the HL funding rate updates 8x faster than CEX rates. By the time Bybit’s funding window closes, HL has already shown the move twice. The current 3.66% APR is moderate. A flip toward zero or negative over the next 5-7 days is your signal that either the flush is finishing or scenario 2 (Fed hike pricing accelerating) is engaging.

Why the S&P is still at 7,408

The S&P printed an all-time high of 7,517.12 on Thursday May 14, then closed Friday at 7,408.50, down 1.45% on the day. The VIX sits at 18.43, below the long-run average of about 19-20. By every measure, equity options are pricing a benign outcome.

A few forces are holding the index up. Concentrated AI capex: a handful of mega-caps have committed hundreds of billions to data-center buildout that short-run earnings revisions can’t touch. Corporate buybacks running at a high pace. And the multiple-expansion bet, which says that if the Fed eventually cuts (even once), the discount rate applied to future earnings falls and valuations re-rate higher on flat earnings.

The bear thesis is the stagflation bind. Sustained oil above $100 plus a hot CPI plus four FOMC dissents takes the “Fed cuts soon” multi-month bet off the table, and without that bet the multiple has to come down. The historical playbook for a stagflation-pricing event in equities is a fast 8-15% drawdown that compresses the multiple in days, not months. You don’t get a slow slide. You get a single-week event.

The 18.43 VIX is what makes the setup interesting: equity options dealers are not hedged for that scenario. If it engages, the move accelerates because dealers have to sell underlying into the decline to maintain delta hedges. VIX above 25 is the line where this loop becomes self-reinforcing.

Three scenarios ahead

Single-point forecasts are useless here. The setup branches.

Scenario 1: Fed cuts in July + ceasefire by July 4 (~25% odds)

The dovish path. CPI rolls over, Warsh as Fed Chair gets political cover for a 25bp cut at the July meeting, Iran negotiations land. This is the equity bull case fully expressed.

  • BTC: retest $95K within 30 days, with a path to $100K+ on a clean break. Funding spikes positive as the long crowd re-engages.
  • S&P: extends to 7,800-8,000. VIX falls to 14-15. The misplaced-euphoria thesis turns out to be correct.
  • VIX: compresses to 14-15.
  • Trade: long BTC-USDC and SP500-USDC on Hyperliquid after a confirming dovish signal. No need for off-chain rate expressions; the 24/7 access is the alpha.

Scenario 2: Fed hikes + war drags (~30% odds)

The stagflation tail. A second hot CPI in June forces the Fed to act. Iran negotiations collapse. Hormuz tightens further. Risk assets sell in tandem.

  • BTC: breaks $70K within 60 days, with $60-65K as the first major support. ETF inflows turn into outflows. Funding flips deeply negative.
  • S&P: -8% to -12% over 4-6 weeks. 6,800 as the first stop, 6,500 on a full unwind.
  • VIX: spikes through 25, prints 30+ on the cleanest days.
  • Trade: short BTC and S&P from breakdown. Long gold against the basket as the stagflation hedge.

Scenario 3: Hawkish hold + frozen war (~45% odds)

The base case if you weight equally. Fed holds, hawkish dissents continue, war stays in chronic state. No catalyst either way.

  • BTC: ranges $73K-$83K for 2-3 months. Funding mean-reverts toward zero. This is a fade-the-extremes tape, not a directional one.
  • S&P: grinds in a 7,200-7,500 range. Each headline either retests or rejects.
  • VIX: ranges 16-22.
  • Trade: tighten position size and only trade the defined range boundaries. Use the Setup B range fade below; don’t hold a directional perp through the chop.

Why on-chain beats CFD and ETF here

Macro events don’t respect market hours. CPI prints at 8:30 AM ET on a Tuesday. The Fed dissent leak appears Saturday afternoon. The Hormuz strike happens Sunday night. The Trump NSC meeting this week is during the day, but the outcome leaks at 11 PM.

The S&P 500 is the constraint. SPY and IVV ETFs close at 4 PM ET Friday and reopen 9:30 AM Monday. CME E-mini futures have a closed period overnight and on weekends. CFD brokers offer extended-hours but spreads widen aggressively during low-liquidity periods, and overnight financing accrues every day you hold. For a position you might hold three weeks waiting for a Fed scenario to resolve, CFD overnight cost compounds to 1-3% of notional.

Hyperliquid runs SP500-USDC as a licensed index perpetual continuously, the same depth at 3 AM Sunday as at noon Wednesday. BTC-USDC trades 24/7 globally. When the news hits, you can act on it.

Cost stack matters more for multi-week macro positions than for day-trades. Hyperliquid charges market-driven funding that can be positive or negative depending on which side is crowded; on flat-funding days the carry is effectively zero. CFD brokers charge a fixed overnight financing every day. Over 30 days, the difference is the whole P/L difference between getting the macro call right and breakeven. Frequently asked questions

What are the odds of a Fed rate hike in 2026?

As of May 18, 2026, Polymarket prices the probability of a Fed rate hike in 2026 at roughly 32%, with resolution by December 9, 2026. CME FedWatch implies a similar one-in-three odds of a 25bp hike by year-end. The repricing followed a hotter-than-expected CPI report on May 12 and four FOMC dissents at the prior meeting, the most dissents since late 1992. Three of those dissents opposed what they called the easing bias of the statement. Incoming Fed Chair Kevin Warsh, historically known as a hawk, has recently signaled a more accommodative stance, creating a structural tension between his stated trajectory and the inflation data the dissenting FOMC members are reacting to.

Why is Bitcoin at $77K and not $90K if institutions are buying?

Two forces are pulling in opposite directions. On the bid side, CoinShares reported $933M of weekly inflows into digital-asset investment products, with 75% of surveyed institutions calling BTC undervalued at current levels. On the offer side, BTC perp funding on Hyperliquid has compressed from roughly 44% APR in early May to 3.66% APR today, meaning the leveraged long crowd is being flushed out. A strong dollar from delayed Fed cuts and rising real yields is the macro headwind. BTC trades like a long-duration risk asset when real yields rise, and that is exactly what is happening.

How does a Fed hike affect Bitcoin and the S&P 500?

A Fed hike is mechanically bearish for both, but through different channels. For the S&P, a hike raises the discount rate applied to future earnings, compressing the multiple investors are willing to pay. For Bitcoin, a hike strengthens the dollar and raises the opportunity cost of holding a non-yielding asset; institutions reallocate at the margin from BTC ETFs into short-duration Treasuries that suddenly yield more. Historically, the equity drawdown is fastest in concentrated AI and tech names; BTC drawdowns lag by 3 to 10 days but tend to be deeper in percentage terms.

What is the stagflation trade on Hyperliquid right now?

Stagflation means inflation stays sticky while growth slows, the worst case for risk assets and the best case for hard assets. The Hyperliquid-native expression is a BTC and S&P pair short, hedged by a gold long via XAUUSDC. If you want a single ticket, short SP500-USDC on a break of 7,350 with a stop at 7,500. The 24/7 venue matters here because Fed and Iran headlines land outside US market hours; futures gap but ETFs cannot be traded. On-chain perps can.

Why does the VIX at 18.43 matter for this setup?

VIX at 18.43 is below the long-run average of around 19-20 and well below the 25 threshold that signals real fear. For a stagflation setup with a 32% Fed-hike risk plus an active war on Day 80, that is unusually calm. It signals the equity options market is not pricing the same downside that the Fed-hike repricing implies for bonds. Historically, VIX moves above 25 happen fast once they trigger. A break above 25 is the cleanest single signal that scenario 2 — Fed hikes plus war drag — is being priced into equities.
