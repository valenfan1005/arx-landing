RWA Perps vs CFD Brokers: The Hidden Cost of Holding

If you hold a gold CFD position overnight on eToro or IG, you pay a swap fee. Every single night. It looks small — 0.01% to 0.03% per day — but compound that over a year and you are paying 3.65% to 10.95% of your position size just for the privilege of holding it. On a $10,000 gold position, that is $365 to $1,095 per year in overnight fees alone. Most CFD traders never calculate this number. They should.

Meanwhile, RWA perpetual contracts on Hyperliquid charge zero overnight fees. The S&P 500 perpetual launched on Hyperliquid in March 2026, licensed by S&P Dow Jones Indices. Gold surpassed $5,000 per ounce. RWA perps now represent over 20% of total DEX open interest. The infrastructure for trading traditional assets on-chain is no longer theoretical — it is live, liquid, and growing fast.

This article compares RWA perpetuals against traditional CFD brokers across fees, custody, access, pricing transparency, and trading hours. The goal is to give you concrete numbers so you can decide whether the switch makes financial sense for your trading style.

What Are RWA Perpetuals?

RWA perpetuals are perpetual futures contracts on real-world assets — gold, silver, crude oil, and the S&P 500 — that trade on decentralized exchanges. They function exactly like crypto perpetuals: no expiry date, USDC-margined, and anchored to the underlying asset price through a funding rate mechanism.

The funding rate is the key difference from overnight fees. Instead of a broker-set charge, it is a market-driven payment between longs and shorts. When more traders are long, longs pay shorts. When more are short, shorts pay longs. The rate fluctuates based on supply and demand, often hovering near zero — and sometimes you get paid to hold your position.

Hyperliquid currently lists perpetual contracts for gold (XAU), silver (XAG), crude oil (WTI), and the S&P 500. The S&P 500 contract is officially licensed by S&P Dow Jones Indices, making it the first licensed S&P 500 derivative on a decentralized exchange. These markets have accumulated over $15B in cumulative volume with more than $2.3B in open interest.

Every trade settles on-chain. Your margin stays in your wallet. There is no broker, no dealing desk, and no counterparty deciding whether to fill your order. The order book is transparent and verifiable by anyone.

What Are CFD Brokers?

A Contract for Difference (CFD) is a derivative that lets you speculate on price movements without owning the underlying asset. When you buy gold on eToro, you do not own gold. You own a contract with eToro where they agree to pay you the difference if gold goes up — and you pay them if it goes down.

The broker is your counterparty. This is the fundamental structural difference. eToro, IG, Plus500, Vantage — they are all on the other side of your trade. They set the spread, they set the overnight fee, and they hold your funds. Some use dealing desks that can reject or re-quote orders during volatility.

CFD brokers generate revenue from three sources: the spread (bid-ask markup), overnight swap fees, and in some cases, direct profit from client losses. Industry data consistently shows that 70-80% of retail CFD accounts lose money — a statistic brokers are required to disclose in many jurisdictions.

The catch is not the spread or the commission. It is the overnight fee — a cost that compounds silently and erodes the profitability of any position held longer than a few hours.

The Real Cost of Overnight Fees

CFD overnight swap fees are calculated based on interbank lending rates (like SOFR or EURIBOR) plus the broker’s markup. The total typically ranges from 0.01% to 0.03% per day, depending on the broker, the asset, and the direction (long or short).

Here is what that looks like in practice:

Example: $10,000 Gold CFD on eToro

  • Daily overnight fee: ~$2–$3
  • Monthly cost: ~$60–$90
  • Annual cost: ~$730–$1,095

That is 7.3% to 10.95% of your position value per year, paid regardless of whether gold goes up or down. If gold returns 12% in a year and you are paying 8% in overnight fees, your net return is 4%. The broker captured two-thirds of your upside.

Example: $10,000 Gold RWA Perp on Hyperliquid

  • Daily funding cost: ~$0–$0.15 (varies by market balance)
  • Monthly cost: ~$0–$5
  • Annual cost: ~$0–$60

The funding rate on RWA perps is market-driven and frequently near zero. During periods of balanced positioning, you may pay nothing. During periods when you are on the minority side, you receive funding payments from the majority side.

Self-Custody vs Broker Custody

When you deposit funds with a CFD broker, the broker holds your money. You are trusting that the broker remains solvent, does not freeze your account, and processes withdrawals when you request them. This is counterparty risk — the same type of risk that wiped out billions when FTX collapsed in 2022.

CFD brokers are regulated (in theory), but regulation varies wildly by jurisdiction. An FCA-regulated broker in the UK offers different protections than an offshore broker in Vanuatu. Even regulated brokers can impose withdrawal delays, freeze accounts during volatility, or become insolvent.

RWA perps on Hyperliquid are non-custodial. Your funds stay in your wallet until you open a position, and your margin is held in a smart contract — not a broker’s bank account. There is no dealing desk that can reject your withdrawal. When you close a position, your USDC is immediately available.

On-chain transparency adds another layer. Every trade, every liquidation, every funding payment is recorded on the blockchain and verifiable by anyone. You do not have to trust the platform’s internal accounting — you can verify it yourself. CFD brokers offer no equivalent transparency into their order execution or counterparty exposure.

24/7 Trading vs Market Hours

Gold CFDs on most brokers trade Monday through Friday with session breaks. The market closes for the weekend, creating gap risk — if a geopolitical event happens on Saturday, you cannot exit your position until Monday. The price may open significantly higher or lower, and your stop-loss may execute at a worse price than you set (slippage through the gap).

RWA perpetuals on Hyperliquid trade 24/7/365. No weekend gaps, no session breaks, no rollovers. If gold moves on a Sunday afternoon because of a central bank announcement, you can react immediately. Your stop-losses execute in real-time, not at Monday’s open price.

This matters most for leveraged positions. A 2% weekend gap on a 10x leveraged gold CFD is a 20% drawdown that you had no ability to manage. On a 24/7 market, you always have the ability to exit, adjust, or hedge.

Transparent Pricing vs Dealing Desk

CFD pricing is set by the broker. The spread you see is the broker’s spread, not the market’s spread. During high volatility — exactly when accurate pricing matters most — brokers can widen spreads, reject orders, or re-quote prices. Some brokers use dealing desk models where they manually decide whether to fill your order or pass it to a liquidity provider.

RWA perps on Hyperliquid trade on an on-chain order book. Prices are determined by actual buy and sell orders from market participants. The spread is visible to everyone. No broker can widen it unilaterally. No dealing desk can reject your order.

Funding rates are equally transparent. On a CFD broker, the overnight fee formula is buried in the terms of service, and the actual rate applied may differ from what is advertised. On Hyperliquid, the funding rate is calculated on-chain, published in real-time, and historical rates are verifiable by anyone. You can see exactly what every trader is paying or receiving.

What are RWA perpetuals?

How much do CFD overnight fees cost per year?
