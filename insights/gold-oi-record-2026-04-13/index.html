Event Summary

Gold perpetual open interest on Hyperliquid surged past $182M as Strait of Hormuz tensions escalated. Silver OI tracked closely at $180M while WTI oil OI reached $1.27B. Despite the aggressive positioning buildup, funding rates remained remarkably flat across all three RWA perps — gold at 0.016%/8h and oil at −0.024%/8h.

Analysis

Geopolitical fear is driving open interest higher, but the positioning is not panicked. Flat funding rates mean longs and shorts are entering in roughly equal proportion — the market is pricing in uncertainty rather than a directional bet. This is a coiling pattern: OI builds pressure while funding stays neutral, then a catalyst forces a directional breakout.

The key signal to watch is funding exceeding 0.01%/8h in either direction. Positive funding above that threshold indicates long crowding (bearish contrarian signal). Negative funding below -0.01%/8h indicates short crowding (bullish contrarian signal). Until then, the market is balanced and waiting for the next geopolitical headline.
